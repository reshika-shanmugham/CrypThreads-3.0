## **EXPERIMENT 1** 

## **Thermal Runaway Propagation & Emergency Battery Isolation Systems** 

## **Procedure** 

1. Open the **Thermal Runaway Propagation & Emergency Battery Isolation System** simulation and load the EV battery pack model. 

2. Enter the required input parameters such as **Initial Battery Temperature, Cell Voltage, Cooling Efficiency,** and **Fault Trigger Time** . 

3. Set the simulation variables within the specified ranges: 

   - Temperature: **25°C – 180°C** 

   - Cooling Efficiency: **40% – 95%** 

   - Fault Duration: **1 s – 20 s** 

4. Run the simulation and observe the temperature distribution across the battery pack under normal operating conditions. 

5. Introduce a short-circuit fault in a selected battery cell at the chosen fault trigger time. 

6. Observe the increase in temperature of the faulty cell and monitor how heat spreads to the surrounding cells. 

7. Note the output parameters displayed during the simulation, including: 

   - Battery Pack Temperature 

   - Isolation Response Time 

   - Number of Affected Cells 

8. Continue the simulation until the faulty cell reaches the safety threshold temperature. 

9. Observe the activation of the **Emergency Battery Isolation System** and study its response to the fault condition. 

10. Analyze how the damaged battery section is isolated from the rest of the battery pack to reduce further heat propagation. 

11. Repeat the simulation by changing the temperature, cooling efficiency, and fault duration values, and compare the resulting thermal behavior. 

12. Record all observations and analyze how different operating conditions affect thermal runaway propagation and the effectiveness of the battery isolation system.
